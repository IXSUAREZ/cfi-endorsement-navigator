PLAIN-ENGLISH EXPLANATION SOURCE OF TRUTH
AC 61-65K endorsements

A.1 - Prerequisites for practical test
Plain-English explanation:
Certifies the applicant completed checkride prep training within the past 2 calendar months and is ready for the practical test.

A.2 - Review of deficiencies identified on airman knowledge test
Plain-English explanation:
Documents that the CFI reviewed the knowledge areas the applicant missed on their written test.

A.3 - Pre-solo aeronautical knowledge
Plain-English explanation:
Confirms the student passed the required pre-solo knowledge quiz for the specific make and model before their first solo.

A.4 - Pre-solo flight training
Plain-English explanation:
Confirms the student has completed and is proficient in all required pre-solo flight training for the specific make and model.

A.5 - Pre-solo flight training at night
Plain-English explanation:
Required before a student solos at night. Covers night procedures at the specific airport and expires 90 calendar days after training.

A.6 - Solo flight (first 90-calendar-day period)
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes a student's first solo flights in a specific make and model. Must be given by the training instructor and expires in 90 calendar days.

A.7 - Solo flight (each additional 90-calendar-day period)
Plain-English explanation:
Renews solo flying authority for another 90 calendar days after the initial endorsement expires.

A.8 - Solo takeoffs and landings at another airport within 25 nautical miles (NM)
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes solo takeoffs and landings at a specific airport within 25 NM. Expires in 90 calendar days.

A.9 - Solo cross-country flight
Plain-English explanation:
Grants the student general solo cross-country authority for a specific category, make, and model.

A.10 - Solo cross-country flight
Plain-English explanation:
Documents the CFI's review of a student's planning for a specific solo cross-country flight. Required before every solo XC flight.

A.11 - Repeated solo cross-country flights not more than 50 NM from the point of departure
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes repeated solo cross-country flights on a specific route no more than 50 NM from the departure airport.

A.12 - Solo flight in Class B airspace
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes a student to fly solo inside Class B airspace after required training. Expires in 90 calendar days.

A.13 - Solo flight to, from, or at an airport located in Class B airspace
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes a student to fly solo to, from, or at a Class B airport after required training. Expires in 90 calendar days.

A.14 - Endorsement of U.S. citizenship recommended by the Transportation Security Administration (TSA)
Plain-English explanation:
Provides the TSA-recommended citizenship endorsement when the CFI chooses the logbook method instead of keeping document copies on file.

A.15 - Solo flight in Class B, C, and D airspace
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes a sport or recreational student to fly solo in Class B, C, or D airspace. Expires in 90 calendar days.

A.16 - Solo flight to, from, or at an airport located in Class B, C, or D airspace or at an airport having an operational control tower
Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes a sport or recreational student to fly solo to, from, or at a towered airport or airport within Class B, C, or D airspace. Expires in 90 calendar days.

A.17 - Aeronautical knowledge test
Plain-English explanation:
Confirms the applicant completed the required ground training and is ready to take the aeronautical knowledge test.

A.18 - Taking flight proficiency check for different category or class of aircraft

Plain-English explanation:
Authorizes a sport pilot to operate in Class B, C, or D airspace or at a towered airport after completing the required training.
